# LinkedIn Carousel Design Playbook 2026

## 1. Cover Slide Hook Formulas
The cover slide must stop the scroll within milliseconds. High-performing hooks act as a filter, attracting the target B2B buyer while signaling immediate value. **Mandatory:** Utilize bold, large, high-contrast typography. All text must reside outside of the mobile "Dead Zones" to prevent UI clipping.

| Framework | Psychological Trigger | Example Opener |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **PAS (Problem-Agitation-Solution)** | High emotional urgency & pain | "Your SaaS churn is killing your growth." |
| **AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)** | Curiosity gap & logic | "The secret to 10k followers in 30 days." |
| **Before / After** | Immediate proof of value | "How we cut design time from 10 hours to 10 minutes." |
| **Mistakes / Loss Aversion** | Authority & risk mitigation | "5 design errors ruining your brand credibility." |
| **Contrarian Take** | Friction, debate, & independent thought | "Stop building features your customers didn't ask for." |

## 2. Optimal Slide Count by Content Type
LinkedIn technically supports 300-page documents, but performance data dictates a rigid 3–10 slide range for maximum completion. Every swipe is a "Depth Score" signal.
*   **Listicles & Checklists:** Mandatory 7–10 slides (one clear item per slide).
*   **Frameworks & Tutorials:** 5–8 slides to maintain velocity.
*   **Case Studies:** 8–10 slides to allow for the narrative arc.
*   **The "Reach King" Range:** 7–10 slides is the strategic sweet spot for consultants and coaches to maximize dwell time without triggering a bounce.

## 3. Text Density and Mobile-First Readability
Rigid constraints are required to pass the AI Quality Classifier and ensure scannability for the 70%+ of users on mobile.

*   **Constraint Checklist:**
    *   **Mandatory:** One clear idea per slide. Do not dilute the focus.
    *   **Paragraph Volume:** Maximum 1–2 sentences. Create white space via aggressive line breaks.
    *   **Typography Scale:** Body text must be **no smaller than 24pt** on a 1080px canvas to ensure mobile legibility.
    *   **AI Slop Prohibition:** Usage of the following phrases is **prohibited** as they trigger down-ranking: *"In today's fast-paced digital landscape," "Delve into,"* and *"Unlock the power of."*

## 4. Swipe Psychology — The "Depth Score" Signals
The 2026 algorithm prioritizes "Depth" (Conversation and Retention) over vanity. 

**The 2026 Depth Score Formula:**  
`(Dwell Time × 2) + (Comment Quality × 15) + (Saves/Shares × 5) - (Bounce Rate)`

*   **Logic:** Comments carry a **15x multiplier** because they represent active "Conversation" rather than passive "Vanity." Your design must facilitate this by asking a specific question that prompts a 5+ word response.
*   **Tactical Commands:** 
    *   **The "See More" Trigger:** The first three lines of the caption must be a standalone hook that forces a click-to-expand.
    *   **Slide 1 Open Loops:** Slide 1 must promise a specific payoff that is only resolved on the final slide.

## 5. CTA Slide Placement and Engagement Ask
The final slide is the "Action Phase." You must choose one primary signal to optimize for:
*   **Saves:** Mandatory ask for educational/utility content. High "Saves" signal to the algorithm that the asset is reference-worthy.
*   **Comments:** Mandatory ask for contrarian/discussion posts to trigger the 15x multiplier.
*   **Human Fingerprint:** For B2B/AI brands, the final slide **must** include personal, candid imagery (e.g., the author at a desk or speaking). This builds human trust and differentiates the asset from generic AI "slop."

## 6. Scroll Stoppers — Knowledge Graph Integration
Carousels are now machine-readable data sources for the LinkedIn Knowledge Graph and AI search scrapers (Perplexity/ChatGPT search).

*   **Direct Answer Blocks:** Mandatory 30–80 word summary block placed near the top of the carousel. This serves as a verifiable source for AI scrapers.
*   **Proprietary Data:** Include original statistics or unique methodologies. These act as "Ground Truth" data that LLMs use as verifiable sources, increasing your citation potential in AI search.

## 7. Typography Scale for B2B Tech/AI
Establish a functional hierarchy using the "AI Generalist" brand kit. 

*   **H1 (Hooks):** Space Grotesk. High-impact, high-contrast.
*   **Body Content:** Inter. Optimized for scannability at 24pt+.
*   **Labels/Data:** JetBrains Mono. Use for technical labels, data points, or "Machine Code" callouts.
*   **Contrast Ratio:** Maintain a minimum 2:1 size ratio between Headline and Body text.

## 8. Color Scheme Patterns: The "Authenticity" Palette
Prohibited: Generic "Corporate Blue" palettes. These contribute to "Strategic Convergence"—the crisis of business sameness where every brand looks identical.

*   **Recommended Palettes:** High-Contrast Minimalist (Authority) or Data-Viz Brutalism (Technical expertise).
*   **Human Fingerprints:** On slides 3–5, you must include "War Stories" or "Hard-won lessons." Injecting proprietary failures or internal field notes prevents your content from being flagged as low-perplexity AI noise.

## 9. Consistency System: The Safe Zone Standard
Design within a 1080x1350px container while respecting the LinkedIn UI overlay.

*   **Mandatory Dead Zones:** Do not place text in the **top 150px** (profile overlay) or **bottom 200px** (CTA/Interaction buttons). 
*   **Margins:** Maintain consistent 75px internal margins.
*   **Progress Indicators:** Page numbers (e.g., 1/10) or progress bars are mandatory to encourage slide completion.

## 10. Technical Specs — Native Document Uploads
Native PDF uploads are the only organic method for swipeable carousels in 2026.

| Spec | Requirement | Reasoning |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Format** | PDF | Only organic method for swipeable rendering. |
| **Dimensions** | 1080 x 1350 px | "The Reach King"—maximizes vertical real estate for 3x dwell time. |
| **File Size** | < 100 MB | Platform limit. |
| **Links** | Bio / Featured Section | **Option B:** External links in the body trigger a 60% reach penalty. |

## Bonus: Decoded Viral Patterns (AI/Tech B2B)
**The "Build in Public" Carousel Flow:**
1.  **Slide 1-2 (The Hook):** Call out a specific failure or operational bottleneck.
2.  **Slide 3-5 (The Fingerprint):** Share "war stories" and the "hard-won lesson" (Source: Hoque). This must include proprietary data or candid photos.
3.  **Slide 6-8 (The Insight):** Reveal the framework or new implementation that solved the problem.
4.  **Slide 9-10 (The Ask):** Specific question to spark a 5+ word comment.
